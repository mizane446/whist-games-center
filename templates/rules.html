Blackjack Rules:

  • The game is played with a standard deck of 52 cards.
  • Each player receives two cards at the beginning of the game.
  • The dealer only reveals one of their cards initially.
  • You can choose to take more cards (hit) or stay with your current cards (stand).
  • If you go over 21 points, you immediately lose (bust).
  • The dealer must hit until they reach at least 17 points.
  • If the dealer busts, you win.
  • If neither player busts, the one with the highest total wins.

Blackjack_eu Rules:

  • The game is played with a standard deck of 52 cards.
  • Each player receives two cards at the beginning of the game.
  • The dealer receives only one card initially (no hole card).
  • You can choose to take more cards (hit) or stay with your current cards (stand).
  • If you go over 21 points, you immediately lose (bust).
  • The dealer must hit until they reach at least 17 points.
  • Dealer checks for blackjack only after you have finished your hand.
  • If neither busts, the one with the highest total wins.

Whist Rules

Whist is a classic 4-player trick-taking card game played with partnerships. It is straightforward to learn but offers deep strategic play. The game is played by two teams: North-South versus East-West.

Objective:

The goal is to win as many tricks as possible. Each team scores one point per trick won. The first team to reach a predetermined score (usually 5 or 7 points) wins the game. In a single deal, the team with more tricks wins that hand.

Setup:

  • Players: 4 players sitting around a table in the positions North, East, South, and West. North and South are partners against East and West.
  • Deck: A standard 52-card deck with no jokers.
  • Deal: All 52 cards are dealt face-down, giving each player exactly 13 cards.
  • Trump: The suit of the last card dealt (turned face-up) becomes the trump suit for the hand. This card is then placed into the dealer's hand.
  • Starting Player: The player to the left of the dealer leads the first trick.

Gameplay & Movement Rules:

  • Following Suit: Players must follow the suit of the card led if they have a card of that suit. If they cannot, they may play any card (including a trump).
  • Winning a Trick: The highest card of the suit led wins the trick. If any trumps are played, the highest trump wins the trick.
  • Leading: The winner of a trick leads the next trick. Any suit may be led.
  • Trump Usage: Trump cards beat all other suits. They are powerful but should be used wisely.
  • Partnership: Partners cannot communicate about their hands. You must rely on strategy and card counting.

Winning the Game:

  • Scoring: Each trick won above 6 counts as one point for the team. (Example: winning 9 tricks = 3 points.)
  • Game End: Play continues until one team reaches the target score (commonly 5 or 7 points). The first team to reach it wins.
  • Single Hand: In this implementation, you play one full deal (13 tricks) and the team with more tricks wins the hand.

Additional Notes:

  • Strategy: Pay attention to what cards have been played, especially high cards and trumps. Try to exhaust your opponents’ cards in a suit before leading it.
  • South Position: You play as South. North is your AI partner, while East and West are your opponents.
  • Card Display: Your cards (South) are shown face-up. The other players’ cards are shown face-down with overlap to save space.
  • Legal Plays: The game will only allow you to select cards that follow the rules (you must follow suit when possible).
